# Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target Customer

### Primary: "Solo Sarah"
- Solo mobile groomer
- 20-30 bookings/week
- Revenue: $60K-100K/year
- Tech comfort: Moderate (uses iPhone, Instagram, Venmo)
- Pain points: No-shows, admin overload, disorganized

### Secondary: "Mike's Mobile Spa"
- 2-5 vans, 3-10 employees
- 100+ bookings/week
- Revenue: $300K-800K/year
- Tech comfort: High (uses Square, QuickBooks)
- Pain points: Scaling, consistency, reporting

---

## Messaging

### Value Proposition
**"Replace spreadsheets, text messages, and missed appointments with one platform built for your business."**

### Key Messages

| Feature | Benefit | Emotional Payoff |
|---------|---------|------------------|
| Online booking | Clients book 24/7 | Freedom from phone tag |
| Deposit collection | 80% fewer no-shows | Peace of mind, protected income |
| Route optimization | Less driving, more grooming | More money, less stress |
| Customer CRM | Remember every pet | Professional reputation |
| Auto reminders | Clients never forget | Reliable schedule |

## Channels

### 1. Content Marketing (SEO)

**Blog Topics:**
- "How to start a mobile dog grooming business"
- "Mobile grooming pricing guide"
- "Best routes for [city] mobile groomers"
- "How to reduce no-shows for groomers"
- "Mobile grooming van setup checklist"

**Target:** Rank for "mobile grooming software" within 6 months

**Cadence:** 2 posts/week

---

### 2. Social Media

**Instagram:**
- Before/after grooming photos
- Day-in-the-life content
- Tips for groomers
- Customer spotlights

**TikTok:**
- Grooming transformations
- "Things only mobile groomers understand"
- Business tips

**Facebook Groups:**
- Mobile Groomers United
- Professional Pet Groomers
- State-specific groomer groups

**Cadence:** Daily posts, engage in comments

---

### 3. Paid Advertising

**Google Ads:**
- Keywords: "mobile grooming software", "grooming booking system", "pet grooming app"
- Budget: $1,000/month starting month 4
- Target CPA: $150

**Facebook/Instagram Ads:**
- Target: Job title "Dog Groomer", "Pet Groomer"
- Interest: Pet grooming, mobile business
- Budget: $500/month starting month 4

---

### 4. Partnerships

**Grooming Schools:**
- Offer student discounts
- Guest lectures on business management
- Sponsor graduation events

**Industry Suppliers:**
- Cross-promote with van outfitters
- Bundle deals with grooming product suppliers

**Associations:**
- National Dog Groomers Association of America
- International Professional Groomers
- State associations

---

### 5. Trade Shows

**Events:**
- GroomExpo (national)
- State grooming conferences
- Pet industry trade shows

**Booth Strategy:**
- Live demo station
- Show special (3 months free)
- Collect emails for follow-up

**Budget:** $10K/year for 2-3 shows

---

### 6. Referral Program

**Offer:**
- Current customer: 1 month free per referral
- New customer: 50% off first month

**Viral Loop:**
- Booking page footer: "Powered by Groomly"
- Email signatures
- Shareable booking links

## Launch Timeline

### Month 1: Soft Launch
- [ ] Beta with 5-10 groomers
- [ ] Collect feedback daily
- [ ] Fix critical bugs
- [ ] Get 3 case studies

### Month 2-3: Content Foundation
- [ ] Publish 10 blog posts
- [ ] Set up social media
- [ ] Create demo video
- [ ] Build email list (lead magnet: "Grooming Business Checklist")

### Month 4-6: Paid Tests
- [ ] Launch Google Ads ($500 budget)
- [ ] Launch FB Ads ($300 budget)
- [ ] Test messaging variations
- [ ] Optimize landing page conversion

### Month 7-12: Scale
- [ ] Double ad budget if CAC < $150
- [ ] Attend first trade show
- [ ] Launch referral program
- [ ] Partnership announcements
